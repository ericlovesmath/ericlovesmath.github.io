A Background

Let’s get one thing clear; while as a programmer I find the concept of the blockchain interesting, NFTs are a stain in the environment and our culture of cyclic exploitation.

Although I could discuss thoroughly on my dislike of NFTs, this is not a post on:

  • The negative environmental impacts of NFTs
  • The regulation of cryptocurrencies
  • The benefits or harms of the blockchain
  • Proof of Work vs Proof of Stake

Instead, this is a discussion on:

  • Whether or not NFTs have had a positive or negative impact on Art
  • Whether or not NFTs should be considered art

What is an NFT?

Note: This will be an heavily simplified explanation, as this post is focused on the art aspect rather than the technical

NFT stands for Non-fungible token

Fungible: Able to replace or be replaced by another identical item; mutually interchangeable.

To put it simply “Non-fungible” is anything that has a unique value to it that isn’t replicable, whether that’s a collector’s edition Pokemon card or an original Van Gogh painting. Typically, a large part of its value comes from the fact that it is unique, rather than its pure utility.

NFTs also have those same traits as Pokemon cards and Van Gogh paintings, except they’re stored on the blockchain. For the purposes of this article, all you have to know about the blockchain is that you can imagine it as series of groups of people checking that the object on the blockchain is real, and passing it on to the next group of people, and so on. Essentially, it artificially creates the scarcity and uniqueness given to NFTs. The NFTs itself are typically in the form of images or videos, which are bought and sold over the blockchain, much like how people trade old baseball cards or coins for a high price.

The process of checking these NFTs is environmentally taxing as well as can be used as a form of money laundering, but this post will only argue against one common argument: “NFTs are the future of art”

The NFT Argument over Art

Comparison to Digital Art

Many in support of NFTs compare it to digital art. After all, a vast amount of NFTs are photos or videos of traditional forms of artwork, or even just works of digital art sold as an NFT. Why should the NFT be seen as less legitimate than such forms of media?

The use of art as artificially inflated sources of money laundering isn’t a new concept, so why should this criticism be made with NFTs?

After all, isn’t the value of art subjective?

The Value of Digital Art vs. NFTs

Its important to recognize that while only some of the art world is surrounded by such controversy, it is almost all of NFTs that are used for the purpose of profit. In fact, I would argue that it is the goal of most NFTs minted today. Low quality and easily generated NFTs are minted every day with the expectation that another buyer will spend a higher price on that NFT, creating one of the largest market bubbles ever seen. The value of the NFTs doesn’t come out of an appreciation of the art itself, but rather for the expectation of profit.

Digital art is almost always commissioned. This means that an individual who recognizes the talent of the artist will pay the artist upfront for their labor, and be given the rights to use the image. This is different from NFTs where the image itself could have been replaced by anything else, and it wouldn’t matter because the value is the concept of owning one rather than legally owning a particular image. If profit is the only motive, the art itself is irrelevant.

NFTs are usually created through generative methods, which means its typically created through the use of a computer program. This does not mean that it is valueless, as plenty of digital artists create generative art to sell as prints or digital products like wallpapers. However, the world of NFTs has diluted generative art into simple program that can mass produce millions of randomized works that hopefully will sell at an absurd price. It’s a slap in the face to artists who use generative art to create the one particular result that they desire.

Many digital artists have now found themselves in a position where using NFTs is the only way to make their ventures profitable, and its being used by pundits to justify the use of NFTs. NFTs are nothing more than the newest beanie baby; no inherit value with a bubble waiting to pop.
