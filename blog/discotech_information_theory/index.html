Need to predict unpredictable events to do so

Predicting Unpredictable Events

Imagine you want be a psychic

  • How will you decide who is good?
  • By testing who is the best at predicting the future

A coin will land heads (1 at $p$) or tails (0 at $1-p$)

How many tosses, and what is your prediction?

  • $A^n \in {0, 1}^n$

    • With 2 flips, $A^2 \in {00, 01, 10, 11}$

  • The prediction must be concise and accurate

    • Accurate 90% of the time: $P(A^n) \geq 0.9$
    • Concise prediction: $\frac{|A^n|}{2^n}$

Running the Simulation

Assume $p = 0.8$, but we must be accurate at least 90% of the time.

  • We keep adding the most likely case to our prediction set until it exceeds 90%
n Prediction Set Accuracy Concision
1 {1, 0} 1.000 1.000
2 {11, 10, 01} 0.960 0.750
3 {111, 110, 101, 011, 100} 0.928 0.625

However, larger n doesn’t mean better

  • $A^{n+1} = {x^n0, x^n1: x^n \in A^n}$
  • $\frac{|A^{n+1}|}{2^{n+1}} = \frac{2|A^n|}{2\cdot 2^n} = \frac{|A^n|}{2^n}$

$$ P(A^{n+1}) = \sum_{x^{n+1} \in A^n} p(x^{n+1}) \ = \sum_{x^n \in A^n} p(x^n0) + p(x^n1) \ $$ $$ = \sum_{x^n \in A^n} p(x^n)\cdot (1-p) + p(x^n)\cdot p \ = \sum_{x^n \in A^n} p(x^n) \ = \boxed{P(A^n)} $$

As n approaches infinity, concision will go to 0
